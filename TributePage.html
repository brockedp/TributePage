Percy Julian

  • Born on April 11, 1899 in Montgomery, AL.
  • Studied at DePauw University majoring in Chemistry graduating valedictorian in 1920.
  • After college, He taught chemistry at Fisk University for two years.
  • He won the Austin Fellowship to Harvard University where he completed a master's degree in organic chemistry
  • In 1929, he went to the University of Vienna, Austria, to begin his doctoral studies of chemistry of medicinal plants
  • He returned to DePauw where he and a Viennese colleague, Josef Pikl, were they accomplished the first total synthesis of physostigmine
  • Physostigmine is used to treat glaucoma.
  • In 1936, he wrote to the Glidden Company in Chicago requesting samples of their soybean oil, in which they hired him on to be their director of research in the Soya Division.
  • Three years after arriving at Glidden, after a mishap with a tank of purified soybean oil, he realized he could produce large amounts of steriods from soybeans such as progesterone.
  • In 1949, Julian found a way to synthesize cortisone inexpensively. He was able to synthesize a related substance from whiich he could them sythesize both hydrocortisone and cortisone.
  • Julian remained at Glidden until 1954, when he founded his own company, Julian Laboratories of Franklin Park, Illinois, and Mexico City.
  • Throughout his life he was socially active in groups seeking to advance conditions for African Americans, helping to found the Legal Defense and Educational Fund of Chicago and serving on the boards of several other organizations and universities.
